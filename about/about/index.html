“QL1” – Built Even by a Beginner Like Me

Aiming to Create a Place Where Even First-Timers Can Take on the Challenge

This website tells the story of a complete beginner trying their hand at programming for the first time.

I especially want those who think “I could never do that” to read this.

Until now, my interaction with digital content and apps was limited to downloading pre-made applications from the App Store and simply using them.

As I supported QL1, I started to wonder—
Is it really enough just to repost relatable content on social media while watching others take action?

In May 2025, I witnessed the beginning of something big: the rise of Deep Thought Computer.
While people with fast access to information, deep knowledge, and quick action were already moving forward, I found myself just watching from the sidelines.
As I followed updates in the Deep Thought Computer Telegram group, I gradually lost the ability to understand what those early movers were talking about.

I thought I could keep up by simply studying while keeping track of the information.
But I soon realized—some things just can’t be understood by passively consuming information.
You can’t get closer just by studying. Action is essential.

Decentralization won’t start if you just wait.

At one point, I paused to really think: What exactly is this QL1 that I chose to support?
And that’s when it hit me—QL1 is seriously aiming to make decentralization a reality.
So, I decided to reflect more deeply on what decentralization actually means.

Category Web2 (Traditional Internet) Web3 (Decentralized Internet)
Data Control Corporations (Google, Meta) Users themselves / the entire network
Apps Centralized servers Decentralized apps (dApps)
Identity ID & password (centralized) Wallets / blockchain accounts

In the end, what truly matters is that each of us takes action in some way.
So I asked myself: What can I do?

This is a chance to learn programming—and maybe even become a developer myself.
If I acquire the right skills, I can contribute to QL1 in a meaningful way.

Let me say this again: I’m a beginner.
I don’t have much influence or reach.
But I do have curiosity—and maybe a bit of courage to take action.

Having never even touched a terminal before, I feel like I’ve stumbled at every single step so far.

And that’s exactly why I want to share this here—
Because maybe, just maybe, it’ll encourage someone else who wants to try, too.
